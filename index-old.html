About Me

I am currently a thir-year Ph.D. student studying computer science under Dr. Liz Bradley at the University of Colorado, Boulder. In 2021 I recieved my Bachelor of Science in computer science and mathematics from Texas State University. My undergraduate thesis was entitled, "The Hidden Shape of Data: Topological Data Analysis for Anxiety Detection in Text," and can be accessed online via the Texas State honors program online thesis library.

Very broadly, my research interests lie in the area of topological data analysis (TDA) for time-evolving metric spaces. A deeper description of my research can be found under my Portfolio section. After completing my Ph.D., I hope to stay on in academia as a professor.

While I was at Texas State, I also had the privilege of being the Digital Product Developer for the student-lead newspaper, The University Star . In this role, I oversaw the management of all of The Star's digital assets, including the management and design of the website and app. I assisted with overhauling the Star's website and ensuring that the new site was WCAG 2.0 compliant. In spring 2021, I earned second place in overall web design at the Texas Intercollegiate Press Association (TIPA).

I enjoy spending any free time I have as far away from technology as possible. My hobbies include reading, crochet, aerial acrobatics, and trying out new baking recipes. A fun fact about myself is that I can recite the first fourty one digits of pi from memory.
