About Me

Currently, I am persuing a Master of Science in computer science at the University of Colorado. In May 2021, I graduated with a Bachelor of Science in computer science and mathematics from Texas State University. My undergraduate thesis was entitled, "The Hidden Shape of Data: Topological Data Analysis for Anxiety Detection in Text," and can be accessed online via the Texas State honors program online thesis library.

I'm still pretty early on in my research career, but I'm interested in the intersection of topological data analysis (TDA) and natural language processing. I'm currently exploring the ways in which we can harness topological tools to probe word embedding models. I believe studying the topology of these embedding spaces can elucidate ways in which these models understand language.

While I was at Texas State, I also had the privilege of being the Digital Product Developer for the student-led newspaper, The University Star. In this role, I oversaw the management of all of The Star's digital assets, including the management and design of the website and app. I assisted with overhauling the Star's website and ensuring that the new site was WCAG 2.0 compliant. In spring 2021, I earned second place in overall web design at the Texas Intercollegiate Press Association (TIPA).

In my free time I like to read, play video games and hang out with my cat, Frank. Since moving to Boulder for grad school, I've also been spending a ton of time hiking, biking, and generall enjoying the outdoors.
